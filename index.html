Oobeo Platform Overview

What Is Oobeo?

Oobeo is a parking operations platform that runs permits, valet, and self-serve transient parking from a single dashboard. One account covers any number of locations with shared reporting, a consolidated customer database, and unified payment processing.

The platform is built for configurability. Rates can be stacked, scheduled, and capped. Validations can be generated in bulk, scoped to specific parking types, and set to auto-rotate codes daily or weekly. Locations can run multiple products simultaneously. Surcharges, taxes, and enforcement integrations are all configured per location. The consumer experience is web based and browser agnostic, so parking customers never need to download an app. Everything from the first customer touchpoint to the final receipt is configurable from the admin dashboard.

This guide covers three Oobeo products:

ProductWhat It Does
PermitsRecurring parking permits with applications, billing, AR, and a self-serve customer portal. Built on a modern React frontend.
ValetFull vehicle lifecycle management (receive, park, request, deliver) with mobile app for attendants and text-based consumer experience.
On-DemandSelf-serve transient parking via QR code or text-to-park. No attendant required.

Key Concepts

AccountThe person or organization that gets billed for a permit(s). If a company buys 50 parking permits for its employees, the company is the Account. If an individual buys one permit for themselves, they are the Account. Every Account has its own invoices, balance, and self-serve portal.
Permit HolderThe individual who actually uses the permit. For a personal permit, the Account and Permit Holder are the same person. For a corporate or group account, the company is the Account and each employee is a Permit Holder.
RateA permit product you sell. A Rate defines the price, how often it bills (monthly, quarterly, yearly), how many permits can be sold (capacity), and any rules like whether applicants need to prove residency. One location can have many Rates. For example: "Monthly Unreserved $150/mo" and "Reserved Spot $250/mo" would be two Rates at the same location.
LocationA physical parking facility: a garage, surface lot, or building. Each Location has its own rates, capacity, and settings. All Oobeo products (Permits, Valet, On-Demand) share the same Location list.
ValidationA discount or free parking rule applied to a transaction. Used primarily in Valet and On-Demand. Can be a code customers enter, a loyalty discount, a VIP rate, restricted to "locals only" with a comma separated list of zip codes, or configured as a time-based rule (e.g., first 30 minutes free).
Share LinkA URL you give to customers so they can apply for a permit. Can be sent by email, text, or printed as a QR code on signage. You can scope a Share Link to a specific location and rate so the customer lands directly on the right application form.
Management PortalA self-serve web page for each Account. This is where permit holders or Account Billing managers pay invoices, view receipts, manage payment methods, and see their permits. Every Account gets a unique portal link that you can copy and send to them.

Options

Controls that apply to all permits at this location:

Limit spaces available for permits — when toggled on, the system enforces a hard cap on the total number of active permits at this location. Once you hit the Max active permits number, no new permits can be sold until one is cancelled. If toggled off, there's no cap and you can oversell.

Location capacity vs. Rate capacity: these are different things. The Location capacity (Max active permits here in Options) is the overall cap for this facility. Rate capacity (Total Spaces in the rate wizard) is the cap for one specific rate product. Example: your garage has 100 total spaces (Location = 100). You sell Monthly Unreserved with 80 spaces and Monthly Reserved with 20 spaces (two rates, 80 + 20 = 100). If you see negative quantities on rates or occupancy over 100%, it usually means the rate capacities add up to more than the location capacity, or the location capacity isn't set.

Physical Permit — turn this on if you hand out physical hangtags, stickers, or cards in addition to the digital permit.

Invoice note — text that appears on every invoice for this location. Use for payment instructions, policy reminders, or contact info.

This location has a 100-permit cap enforced. Physical Permits are off. Invoice note is blank but you could add something like "Contact parking@building.com with questions."

Click + Create Rate to launch the 4-step wizard:

Step 1: Location & Basic Info

Pick which location this rate belongs to. Name it something customers will understand (e.g., "Monthly Unreserved" or "Employee Quarterly"). Set the Total Spaces, which is the cap on how many permits can be sold under this rate. General Ledger Code is optional and used for accounting exports (e.g., mapping to a revenue code in NetSuite).

Step 1: The Permit Type (Name) is what your customers see. Total Spaces = 1 would mean only one person can hold this rate at a time.

Step 2: Pricing & Schedule

Set the price. The Allow Prorations toggle controls whether a customer who starts mid-cycle gets a prorated first invoice or pays the full amount. For example: if someone buys a $100/month permit on March 15th, prorations on means they pay ~$50 for the rest of March, then $100 starting April. Prorations off means they pay $100 regardless of start date.

Prorations only apply to the first invoice. All subsequent invoices are the full rate amount. Make sure this toggle is set correctly before selling permits. If a customer reports being charged full price when they expected a prorated amount, check this setting on the rate.

Setup Fee is a one-time charge added to the first invoice only. Use it for key deposits, access card fees, or administrative charges. It appears as a separate line item on the invoice so the customer sees exactly what they're paying for.

Invoice Interval determines billing frequency. Monthly is most common. Quarterly bills every 3 months (e.g., Jan 1, Apr 1, Jul 1, Oct 1). Yearly bills once per year. Seasonal

For Quarterly billing, invoices are generated at the start of each quarter. If a customer's due date looks wrong (e.g., showing April 15 when you expected March 15), check whether the invoice was generated for the current quarter or the next one.

Schedule Valid Times lets you restrict when the permit is active. "Always valid" means 24/7 access. Click "Manage Schedules" to see existing schedules or create new ones.

The schedule library. "Always valid" means no time restrictions. "Custom schedule" means specific hours have been set. You can reuse schedules across multiple rates. Click Edit to modify, or + New Schedule to create one.

Common Invoice Situations

Making an adjustment: Use the Edit button to modify an invoice amount. If you just need to mark it as resolved without an actual payment (e.g., writing off a small balance or applying a courtesy credit), use Edit to adjust the amount, or go to the Account and use Add Credit to offset the balance.

Cancelled permit but invoice still open: Cancelling a permit does not automatically void unpaid invoices for the current period. You'll need to manually Edit or Refund the invoice if the customer shouldn't owe for that period.

Adding permits to an existing account: If you add permits to an account mid-cycle using Edit Quantity, the system generates a new invoice covering the additional permits. Be aware that the new invoice reflects the updated total, not just the incremental change. Review the invoice to make sure it looks right before sending.

What customers see on the payment link: When a customer clicks the payment link from their email, they see the total amount due and a Pay Now button. The payment page does not currently show a line-item breakdown. If customers ask why they owe a certain amount, direct them to their Account Portal where they can View Receipts for the full detail, or resend the itemized invoice PDF.

AR Workflow

A typical accounts receivable cycle:

  1. Go to Invoices tab, click Show Unpaid Only to see all outstanding balances
  2. Click into each invoice to see the details and how long it's been unpaid
  3. Use Resend for a first nudge, or Communications for a custom message ("Hi Jane, your March invoice is 15 days past due...")
  4. Check the account's Activity log to see if they've received previous reminders
  5. If still unresolved, open the permit and use Suspend Permit as a next step. The customer's portal will show a "Suspended" warning encouraging them to pay.

Applications & Share Links

Reviewing Applications

When someone applies for a permit through a share link or the Parking Finder, their application lands in a review queue. The summary bar at the top of the dashboard shows the count (e.g., "Applications: 4 View"). Click "View" to see pending applications.

For each application, you can Approve (which creates the permit, generates the first invoice, and notifies the customer), Reject (sends a rejection notice), or Request more info (asks the applicant for additional details before you decide).

If you don't want to manually review every application, turn on "Auto Approve Applicants" in the rate wizard (Step 3). Applications on that rate will be approved instantly. This is useful for high-volume, general public rates. Don't use it for rates where you need to verify eligibility (employee rates, resident rates, etc.).

Things to Know

You can't edit your own account info from the portal. If you need to change your name, email, phone number, or address, contact your parking operator and they'll update it for you.

When paying online, you pay the full balance due. The payment page shows your total across all unpaid invoices. You can't select individual invoices to pay separately.

The payment link in your email shows the total, not a line-item breakdown. To see a detailed breakdown of what you owe, log into your Account Portal and click View Receipts, or ask your operator to resend the invoice PDF.

FAQ

Rates & Configuration

What's the difference between Location capacity and Rate capacity?

Location capacity (set in Location > Options > Max active permits) is the total cap for the entire facility. Rate capacity (set in Total Spaces when creating a rate) is the cap for that one rate product. You can have multiple rates at one location whose capacities add up to the location cap. See the Locations > Options section for details.

Why does my rate show as Private when I set it to Public?

Two things must be true for a rate to appear on the public application form: the "Public Rate" toggle must be on (Rate wizard Step 3), AND the rate must be Active (not Paused). Check both settings.

How do I set up auto-approval?

In the rate wizard Step 3 (Options & Requirements), toggle on "Auto Approve Applicants." Applications under that rate will be approved and activated immediately with no manual review.

Why didn't proration work on a mid-month permit?

Check whether "Allow Prorations" is toggled on for that rate (Rate wizard Step 2). If it's off, the customer pays full price regardless of when they start. Prorations only apply to the first invoice.

Accounts & Permits

How do I cancel a permit?

Open the permit detail. Four options: Suspend (temporary, reversible), Cancel at Renewal (active until period ends), Cancel on Date (pick a date), or Cancel Immediately. Most common is Cancel at Renewal.

I cancelled a permit but the invoice is still open. Is that right?

Yes. Cancelling a permit does not automatically void the current period's invoice. If the customer shouldn't owe for that period, you need to manually Edit or Refund the invoice.

How do I record a cash or check payment?

Open the Account detail and click Take Payment, or open the specific invoice. Select the payment method and record the amount.

How do I adjust an invoice amount?

Open the invoice and click Edit to change the amount. You can also go to the Account and use Add/Subtract Credit to offset a balance without modifying the invoice itself.

Why is occupancy over 100%?

More active permits exist than the capacity allows. This happens when the "Limit spaces" toggle is off in Location > Options, or when rate capacities weren't aligned with the location cap. Review both the location-level and rate-level capacity settings.

Customers

How do I pay my balance?

Click Pay Now on your Account Portal, or use the payment link in your invoice email. Both take you to a payment page where you can pay by credit card.

Can I pay just one invoice instead of my full balance?

Currently, the payment page shows your total balance due. You cannot select individual invoices to pay separately. If you need to make a partial payment, contact your parking operator.

How do I change my vehicle?

Contact your parking operator. They can update the vehicle on your permit from the admin dashboard. You cannot change vehicle information from the customer portal.

How do I update my name, email, or address?

Contact your parking operator. Account information can only be edited by the operator from the admin dashboard. You cannot edit your own account details from the portal.

Why doesn't my payment link show what I'm paying for?

The payment page shows your total due but not a line-item breakdown. For the detailed breakdown, check your Account Portal under View Receipts, or ask your operator to resend the invoice as a PDF.
